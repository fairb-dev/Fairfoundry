### OEM-38: Elysian Beauty Holdings

**Industry:** Personal Care & Cosmetics -- Skincare, Sunscreen, Color Cosmetics

**Headquarters:** Paris, France (R&D in Grasse, France and Singapore)

**Annual Revenue:** $4.8B

**Number of Supplier Relationships:** 220

**Products They Source:**
- Sunscreen formulations (SPF 30-50+, broad spectrum, mineral and chemical UV filters)
- Preservative systems for aqueous cosmetic products (paraben-free, "clean beauty" formulations)
- Color cosmetic raw materials (pigments, micas, iron oxides)
- Packaging components (glass jars, airless pumps, aluminum tubes)
- Fragrance compounds (natural essential oils and synthetic aroma chemicals)
- Active ingredients (retinol, niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, peptides)

**Typical ERS Structure:**
- Product performance specifications (SPF, UVA-PF, water resistance) per FDA 21 CFR 201.327 and EU Cosmetics Regulation (EC) No 1223/2009
- Preservative efficacy requirements per USP <51> (US market) and ISO 11930 (EU market)
- Stability testing protocol per ICH Q1A adapted for cosmetics (25 C/60% RH for 24 months, 40 C/75% RH for 6 months accelerated)
- Microbiological limits per ISO 17516: aerobic mesophilic bacteria <= 1000 CFU/g, absence of specified pathogens
- Heavy metal limits per EU Cosmetics Regulation: Lead <= 10 ppm, Arsenic <= 2 ppm, Mercury <= 1 ppm, Cadmium <= 5 ppm
- Test data format: PDF study reports (regulatory standard), with raw data tables in Excel

**Quality Requirements:**
- cGMP per ISO 22716:2007 (Cosmetics -- Good Manufacturing Practices)
- EU CPNP notification for all products sold in EU
- FDA OTC drug registration for sunscreen products
- Vegan/cruelty-free certification (Leaping Bunny) for all new launches
- COSMOS certification for organic product lines

**Payment Terms:** Net-45 from invoice date; progress payments for custom formulation development projects

**Pain Points:**
- SPF testing costs $4,000-$8,000 per formulation and takes 3-4 weeks. Re-formulation and re-testing cycles can consume $50K+ before a single product launches. No systematic way to correlate in vitro UV absorbance data with in vivo SPF results across different testing labs.
- Preservative efficacy testing takes 28 days (USP <51>) with binary pass/fail outcome. Multiple reformulations may be needed, each requiring a full 28-day test cycle. Elysian has no predictive model linking preservative concentration to challenge test outcome.
- Contract testing laboratories in Singapore (for ASEAN market) and France (for EU market) use slightly different strains and protocols, leading to occasional discrepancies in preservative efficacy results for the same formulation.

**Why They'd Use Fairbuild:**
- Centralized test data repository would enable correlation analysis across SPF test labs, reducing re-test costs
- Immutable challenge test records would eliminate disputes between formulation team and QA over preservative efficacy results
- Escrow-based payment for contract testing services would ensure timely report delivery

### OEM-39: Prairie Grain Cooperative

**Industry:** Agriculture -- Grain Handling, Storage, and Marketing

**Headquarters:** Wichita, Kansas, USA (120 country elevators across Kansas, Oklahoma, Colorado)

**Annual Revenue:** $6.2B (cooperative revenue, not profit)

**Number of Supplier Relationships:** 15,000 (farmer-members) + 85 service providers

**Products They Source (and sell):**
- Hard red winter wheat (primary crop, 40% of volume)
- Corn (30% of volume)
- Grain sorghum (milo, 15% of volume)
- Soybeans (15% of volume)
- Grain inspection and grading services (outsourced to licensed agencies)
- Grain fumigation and pest control services
- Railcar leasing and logistics services

**Typical ERS Structure:**
- Grain purchase contracts reference USDA Official Grain Grading Standards (7 CFR 810)
- Quality specifications for export contracts per GAFTA (Grain and Feed Trade Association) or NAEGA (North American Export Grain Association) standard forms
- Test methods per AACC International Approved Methods (moisture, protein, falling number, test weight)
- Mycotoxin limits per FDA Action Levels: DON (vomitoxin) <= 1 ppm for finished flour, aflatoxin <= 20 ppb
- Data format: GIPSA/FGIS official certificates (paper, transitioning to electronic)

**Quality Requirements:**
- GIPSA/FGIS licensed inspection at export elevators
- FSMA (Food Safety Modernization Act) compliance for grain storage facilities
- GMP+ B3 certification for animal feed ingredients
- Mycotoxin monitoring per FDA Compliance Policy Guides

**Payment Terms:** Cash on delivery at country elevator (basis local cash price); futures contract settlement T+2

**Pain Points:**
- Protein content determines price premium/discount at $0.10-$0.30/bushel per percentage point. A 0.3% protein measurement difference between elevator NIRT instrument and export terminal laboratory instrument on a 100,000-bushel train can mean a $30,000 pricing discrepancy.
- 120 country elevators each have their own NIRT instrument (Perten, FOSS, or Bruker). Cross-instrument calibration is managed annually, but drift between calibration events can cause systematic measurement bias.
- Farmer disputes over grade at the elevator scale house are the single largest source of member complaints (2,200 grade disputes per harvest season). No immutable shared record of the grade determination.

**Why They'd Use Fairbuild:**
- Immutable grade record at point of delivery would reduce farmer disputes from 2,200 to near-zero
- Cross-instrument NIRT calibration monitoring via shared test data platform
- Export contract quality documentation on shared ledger would reduce letter of credit disputes

### OEM-40: AgriNutrient Solutions Corp

**Industry:** Agricultural Chemicals -- Fertilizer Manufacturing and Distribution

**Headquarters:** Omaha, Nebraska, USA

**Annual Revenue:** $2.1B

**Number of Supplier Relationships:** 45 (raw material suppliers) + 180 (contract blending and testing)

**Products They Source/Manufacture:**
- Granular NPK fertilizer blends (various formulations: 10-10-10, 15-15-15, 18-46-0 DAP, 0-0-60 MOP)
- Controlled-release nitrogen fertilizers (polymer-coated urea)
- Micronutrient blends (zinc, boron, manganese, iron sulfate)
- Liquid fertilizer solutions (UAN 28-0-0, 32-0-0)
- Soil amendment products (gypsum, lime, sulfur)

**Typical ERS Structure:**
- Nutrient guarantee per AAPFCO Uniform State Fertilizer Bill
- Analytical methods per AOAC Official Methods of Analysis
- Heavy metal limits per AAPFCO SUIP #25
- Physical quality: SGN, Uniformity Index, crushing strength per TFI guidelines
- Label compliance per state department of agriculture registration requirements

**Quality Requirements:**
- AAPFCO compliance for all 50 states
- EPA registration for pesticide-fertilizer combination products
- State fertilizer tonnage reporting
- Annual plant inspection by state department of agriculture
- AOAC proficiency testing program participation

**Payment Terms:** Net-30 for raw materials; COD for custom blend orders

**Pain Points:**
- State inspectors can pull samples from any retail location. If a sample fails the AAPFCO investigative allowance, a stop-sale order is issued and AgriNutrient must re-blend or re-label the lot. Stop-sale events cost $15,000-$50,000 in logistics and re-processing.
- Seasonal demand peaks (spring planting) require rapid blending and testing turnaround. Contract laboratories often have 2-week backlogs during March-April, delaying quality certification and shipment.
- No systematic way to correlate blending process parameters (blend time, ingredient lot, moisture) with nutrient analysis results. Quality issues are discovered after the fact via state inspection.

**Why They'd Use Fairbuild:**
- Real-time nutrient analysis tracking from blending line to laboratory to certificate would eliminate stop-sale events
- Shared data platform with contract laboratories would reduce reporting turnaround from 2 weeks to days
- Traceability from ingredient lot through blending to final product would enable root cause analysis for nutrient deficiencies
