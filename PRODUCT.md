# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fairbuild Studio is for enterprise manufacturing, quality, procurement, and operations teams evaluating whether factory production logs can drive contract automation. A typical evaluator has a CSV or JSON production log, an engineering requirements specification, and a need to understand quickly whether Fairbuild can turn that evidence into pass/fail gates, quality grades, review holds, and settlement-instruction previews.

## Product Purpose

The Studio sandbox proves the core Fairbuild workflow in minutes: load a manufacturing log, extract and classify production parameters, map those parameters to ERS gates, grade units, identify review holds, and produce an auditable settlement-instruction preview. Success means a buyer can understand the workflow without a sales call, then contact Fairbuild with confidence that the product can remove friction between OEMs and factories.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, enterprise-ready. The product should feel like serious infrastructure for manufacturing contracts, not a marketing demo or a toy analytics dashboard.

## Anti-references

Avoid high-school project styling, generic SaaS card grids, decorative crypto aesthetics, gratuitous gradients, tiny table controls, engineering diagrams as primary marketing, and dashboards that expose all data before explaining the business outcome.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the operational outcome before the raw table.
2. Make the 20-minute sandbox path obvious: load, extract, gate, grade, review, report.
3. Treat dense manufacturing evidence as a professional work surface with strong contrast, predictable controls, and contained tables.
4. Keep custody and payment boundaries explicit: Fairbuild issues settlement-instruction previews in sandbox mode and does not provide production custody, escrow, or payment services here.
5. Design for enterprise trust: stable layouts, readable typography, restrained color, accessible controls, and no ornamental effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain 4.5:1 contrast for body text and table headers, 44px touch targets on mobile and primary controls, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, keyboard navigation, and layouts that do not create document-level horizontal scrolling.
